Philippines Pork Adobo

Ingredients

Pork Belly: 1 KG

Soy Sauce: 1/2 cup

Garlic: 5 cloves

Bay Leaves: 2

Vinegar: 1/4 cup

Pepper: to taste

Instructions

  1. Cut the pork belly into 1-inch thick pieces.
  2. (Optional but if you want it to be authentic) Combine the pork belly, soy sauce, and garlic, then marinate for at least 1 hour.
  3. Heat the pot and add the pork belly. Cook until golden brown.
  4. Set aside the pork.
  5. In the same pot, sauté the garlic until fragrant.
  6. Add the pork back into the pot. Add soy sauce, pepper, and enough water to cover the pork. Bring to a boil.
  7. Once boiling, add the vinegar. Do not stir. Let it cook for about 5 minutes.
  8. Add the bay leaves and simmer for 10 minutes or until the sauce thickens.
  9. Serve with hot rice and enjoy!

Origin of Adobo

The story of adobo begins long before Spanish colonization, rooted in the indigenous practices of the Philippines. The word “adobo” is derived from the Spanish term adobar, which means “to marinate.” However, the dish itself existed even before Spanish influence reached the archipelago. Early Filipinos relied on vinegar and salt to preserve meat, especially in the tropical climate where food spoils quickly. This simple preservation method not only kept food edible but also infused it with a tangy, savoury flavour that would later define adobo’s iconic taste. When the Spanish arrived in the 16th century, they introduced soy sauce, garlic, and other spices, further enhancing the dish’s flavor profile. Over time, the combination of indigenous techniques and Spanish influences transformed adobo into a uniquely Filipino dish.
