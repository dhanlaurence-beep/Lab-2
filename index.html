Philippines Pork Adobo

Ingredients

  • Pork Belly: 1 KG
  • Soy Sauce: 1/2 cup
  • Garlic: 5 cloves
  • Bay Leaves: 2
  • Vinegar: 1/4 cup
  • Pepper: to taste

Instructions

  1. Cut the pork belly into 1-inch thick pieces.
  2. (Optional but authentic) Combine the pork belly, soy sauce, and garlic, then marinate for at least 1 hour.
  3. Heat the pot and add the pork belly. Cook until golden brown.
  4. Set aside the pork.
  5. In the same pot, sauté the garlic until fragrant.
  6. Add the pork back into the pot. Add soy sauce, pepper, and enough water to cover the pork. Bring to a boil.
  7. Once boiling, add the vinegar. Do not stir. Let it cook for about 5 minutes.
  8. Add the bay leaves and simmer for 10 minutes or until the sauce thickens.
  9. Serve with hot rice and enjoy!

Origin of Adobo

Adobo began as an indigenous Filipino method of preserving meat using vinegar and salt, long before Spanish colonization. This technique protected food in the tropical climate while creating the tangy, savory flavor that defines adobo today. When the Spanish arrived in the 16th century, they introduced the term adobar (“to marinate”) and ingredients like soy sauce and garlic. Over time, these influences blended with local practices, shaping adobo into a uniquely Filipino dish that reflects the country’s resourcefulness and cultural diversity.

Pork or chicken adobo is one of the most popular dishes in the Philippines. It is a must-try food in Filipino cuisine, especially for visitors experiencing the country for the first time.
